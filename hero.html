Act 01
What your hero section needs to do
Four decisions. Every Hero website that converts makes all four of them the same way.
Hero section direction
Visual
Dark background, bold amber accents. High-contrast hero image or no image at all — just words that hit. Nothing decorative.
Headline
Short, direct, assumes capability. No questions — a statement. "Stop waiting. Start winning." energy. Active voice, always.
The proof point
One number, one result, one before-and-after. Not a list — a single thing that makes them stop and think: this person is serious.
CTA
Your CTA is a decision, not an invitation. "Start now." "Get the plan." Never "Learn more" — that's a browser's button, not yours.
Every visual and verbal decision — made for The Hero. Use this as your brief for everything.

Act 03
Your brand in one paragraph.
Paste this at the start of any AI prompt — ChatGPT, Claude, whatever you use. Every caption, email, and sales page you write from here will sound like you.
Copy-ready AI context block
My brand archetype is The Hero (The War Room). My brand voice is direct, confident, and results-focused. I use short, punchy sentences. Active voice always. I assume capability in my audience — no hand-holding, no hedging, no filler. My visual identity uses dark backgrounds (#1c1917), amber accents (#f59e0b), and cream (#fef3c7). Sharp corners everywhere, tight purposeful whitespace, and animations that move upward. My typography pairs a heavy serif for headlines with a clean sans-serif for body. My brand energy feels like a starting gun — earned, not given. When writing for my brand, channel the energy of someone who has done the work and is here to help others do the same. No excuses, no fluff, just results.
