Typography
Your fonts should feel like the packaging on something you'd find at a carnival. Bold enough to see from across the room, quirky enough to make you smile. Headlines in a chunky expressive serif. Body text in a personality-forward sans-serif that has opinions. Think Ben & Jerry's - the fonts are part of the joke.
Voice and tone
Warm, playful, genuinely funny. Not trying-to-be-funny - actually funny. You make the technical approachable by treating it like the absurd thing it actually is. No jargon, no corporate speak. Your copy should make people snort-laugh at their desk.
The thing that makes a Jester brand different
Joy is a strategy, not a style choice. While everyone else is being Very Serious About Their Business, you're having fun - and your clients are having fun with you. That's not unprofessional. That's a competitive advantage nobody else thought to use.

Things wobble and bounce - like they're happy to see you. Buttons tilt when you hover. Cards wiggle. The page should feel alive and slightly unhinged. In a good way. Notice how this card reacted when you hovered it.
