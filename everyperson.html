Typography
Friendly, approachable, nothing pretentious. Headlines in a warm, slightly rounded serif that feels like a conversation, not a presentation. Body text in a readable sans-serif that doesn't try too hard. Think Mailchimp's typography - professional but human. Fonts that feel like they were chosen by a real person, not a branding agency.
Voice and tone
Warm, genuine, like talking to a friend. Use contractions. Make small jokes. Be specific about what you do without making it sound bigger than it is. Your copy should feel like a text from someone you trust - direct, honest, maybe a little self-deprecating. No corporate speak. No 'leveraging synergies'. Just real words.
The thing that makes a Everyperson brand different
Most brands try to impress. You try to connect. That sounds simple but it's actually rare - because connecting requires being genuinely yourself rather than a polished version of what you think people want. The brands people love most aren't the flashiest ones. They're the ones that made them feel at home.
What your website actually looks and feels like
Corners
Slightly rounded - just a small softening, like a well-worn business card. Friendly but not childish. 6px border radius. Not sharp, not bubbly. Just comfortable.
Animation - hover this
Subtle and functional - content fades in gently as you scroll, nothing dramatic. The page shouldn't feel like it's performing. It should feel like it's just there, ready to be read.
Voice
Write the way you talk. Warm, direct, unpretentious. Use contractions. Make jokes that land. No jargon. Your copy should feel like a text from a friend who also happens to be really good at what they do.
Section flow
Warm and welcoming throughout - soft greens and warm whites that feel like someone's well-designed living room. One darker section to add depth, then back to comfort. The page shouldn't feel like a performance - it should feel like you're already there, sitting down, getting comfortable.
