Typography
Soft, warm, readable. Your headlines should feel like a gentle exhale - a rounded serif with weight but no sharpness. Body text open and easy on the eyes, generous line spacing. Think of a beautifully designed wellness brand - fonts that feel like they have time for you. Nothing aggressive, nothing that shouts.
Voice and tone
Warm, personal, unhurried. You speak to one person, not an audience. Your copy should feel like sitting across the table from someone who genuinely cares. Use 'you' more than 'we'. Ask questions. Acknowledge feelings before jumping to solutions. Your words should feel like a hand on someone's shoulder.
The thing that makes a Caregiver brand different
Most brands talk about what they do. You talk about how people feel. That's not a soft version of business - it's the whole point. The clients who need you most are looking for safety before they can even think about results. You give them that without trying.
What your website actually looks and feels like
Corners
Softly rounded - like the corner of a well-used greeting card. 8-12px border radius. Approachable, safe, held. Sharp corners would feel too clinical for someone who makes people feel cared for.
Animation
Very gentle fades and drifts - like a door opening slowly. Nothing bounces or pops. Everything eases in as if it has time for you. The pace of the page should feel unhurried.
Photos
Warm, personal, human. Genuine moments over staged shots. Photos where you can feel the care in them. If it feels like a stock photo of someone looking helpful, don't use it.
Section flow
Light and warm throughout - soft pinks and warm whites that feel like morning light. One darker section to show depth and credibility, then back to warmth. The page should feel like walking into a room where someone left the lamp on for you.
