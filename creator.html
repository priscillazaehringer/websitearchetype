Typography
Expressive, distinctive, impossible to confuse with anyone else. Headlines in a bold serif or display font with real character - not a font that came free with a template. Body text clean but with personality. Think Pentagram's portfolio - the typography IS the design. Your fonts should feel like they were chosen by someone with strong opinions.
Voice and tone
Confident, specific, unapologetically original. You don't describe what you do in generic terms - you describe it in a way that could only come from you. Your copy should feel like an artist's statement, not a service page. Strong point of view. Personal references. Language that has a signature.
The thing that makes a Creator brand different
Generic is the enemy. You don't just deliver work - you deliver your work. The reason clients come back isn't just quality, it's the specific quality that only you produce. Your brand's job is to make that specificity visible before they even reach out.
What your website actually looks and feels like
Corners
Sharp and precise - craft is intentional. Every edge is chosen, not defaulted to. Sharp corners say I made a decision here. This isn't about being cold - it's about being deliberate.
Animation - hover this
Things arrive with intention - like watching someone write a word rather than seeing it appear. On hover this card tilts and shifts. A little unpredictable, a little alive. Considered chaos.
Photos
Behind the scenes and process - show the work being made, not just finished. Sketches, tools, works in progress. The process IS the proof of your originality.
Section flow
Dark cinematic base, then bold color explosions, then breathing room. The rhythm should feel like a gallery opening - dramatic entrance, moments of intensity, quiet spaces to reflect. Never predictable, always intentional.
