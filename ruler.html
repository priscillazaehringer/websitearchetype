The thing that makes a Ruler brand different
Most brands ask for trust. You command it. There's a difference between saying "you can count on me" and making someone feel - from the moment they land on your site - that of course they can count on you. That certainty isn't arrogance. It's what your clients are paying for.
What your website actually looks and feels like
Corners
Perfectly sharp everywhere. Think a business card with laser-cut edges. Every sharp corner signals precision and intentionality. Softness here would read as uncertainty.
Animation
Nothing moves. Stillness communicates authority - movement would cheapen it. The page should feel like a gallery. That is the maximum acceptable animation for a Ruler brand.
White space
Maximum. Empty space is a statement. Like a high-end store that displays 3 items. The space says what's here is important enough to stand alone.
